I’m a real-world evidence (RWE) and AI scientist working in oncology drug development. I’m an RWE Associate Director at AstraZeneca in Barcelona. I work on real-world epidemiology across women’s cancers — breast, ovarian, and endometrial — for clinical trial planning, regulatory agency submission, and health technology assessment (HTA).

Most of my work is about generating regulatory-relevant evidence from electronic health record and claims data: designing and delivering observational studies and external control arms, and applying causal inference methods — target trial emulation, propensity-score matching, IPTW, clone-censor-weight — to contextualise clinical trials and inform investment and regulatory decisions. I work on the applied use of AI in evidence generation: automating as much as possible, and designing AI-ready data pipelines, internal tooling, and agentic frameworks.

Before industry, I was a post-doctoral data analyst working on the COVID response in Scotland. I hold a PhD from the University of Edinburgh in Ecology and Evolutionary Biology, where I worked on wild wood mice: social network analysis, parasite epidemiology, and statistical modelling.

I’ve published in *The Lancet Respiratory Medicine*, *Nature Communications*, *The Lancet Regional Health – Europe*, and *JCO Precision Oncology*, and I work primarily in R.
