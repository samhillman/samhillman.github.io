I’m a real-world evidence (RWE) and AI scientist working in oncology drug development. I’m an RWE Associate Director at AstraZeneca in Barcelona, within Clinical Data Sciences in Oncology Data Science and AI, where I lead real-world epidemiology across women’s cancers — breast, ovarian, and endometrial.

Most of my work is about generating regulatory-relevant evidence from electronic health record and claims data: designing and delivering observational studies and external control arms, and applying causal inference methods — target trial emulation, propensity-score matching, IPTW, clone-censor-weight — to contextualise clinical trials and inform investment and regulatory decisions.

Alongside that, I work on the applied use of AI in evidence generation. I co-designed an end-to-end agentic pipeline for automated RWE analyses, and I lead AI-development training for my team. I hold a PhD from the University of Edinburgh on resource-mediated effects in wild wood mouse networks, which combined fieldwork with social network analysis, parasite epidemiology, and statistical modelling — the observational-data problems turn out to rhyme more than you’d expect.

I’ve published in *The Lancet Respiratory Medicine*, *Nature Communications*, *The Lancet Regional Health – Europe*, and *JCO Precision Oncology*, and I work primarily in R.
